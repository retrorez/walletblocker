How WalletBlocker Anti-Theft Cards Works

Active RFID blocking intercepts the credit card reader's transmitted signal power, and sending back scrambled information, thereby preventing the microchip in the RFID credit/debit card working, this means your data is safe. Essentially RFID readers transmit signals which power the microchip in the card, the microchip in the card powers up and sends data back to the reader. We prevent this by transmitting a signal that interferes with the reader but this only works with one frequency and often a certain type of technology.

Contactless payment cards, ID cards, passports all use the same basic technology, Radio Frequencies IDentification otherwise known as (RFID). Inside your bankcard, hidden in that thin card, is a microchip linked to an antenna, passport or security pass. The cards and passports use a standard set of radio frequencies. All bank contactless payment cards use 13.56 Mhz. Security passes, keys, product tags typically use 125 kHz as well as 13.56 Mhz.

Most RFID systems are designed to work at short-range, typically only a few inches. However credit/debit cards can be read by scanners with powerful RFID readers and the real concern 30+ meters by hackers.
